GAN-generated stock photos of salads.
DeepStock, also called "Deep Pix2Stock Salad," is a series of generative stock photos of salads, exploring issues of originality and ownership in online content creation. We began with a dataset of 2134 watermarked images of salads from Shutterstock. We trained an existing Pytorch representation of DCGAN on our dataset for 100 epochs (a timelapse of its progress is shown below). To improve the resolution of the resulting images, we created a Pix2Pix model and trained it for 20 epochs where the A images were low-res versions of the pictures in our dataset, and their corresponding B images were the high-res versions. Then we used this model to sharpen and texturize our generated salad photos. The final photos (some shown below) had much more clearly defined bowls, leaves, and watermarks.

DeepStock was made as part of a group project for the course Art and Machine Learning. I was in charge of generating salad photos with the method described above (the project also included other generation techniques). Our full documentation is available here.
