A messaging app that translates all text into rebus puzzles (picture puns).
Rebus Chat is an Android messaging app made using Android Studio and Firebase. The critical part of the project is the rebus-ification algorithm, which basically does the following: convert the message into a series of phonemes using the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary, check the bank of around 150 named icons, and use dynamic programming to find the sequence of icons and letters that best matches the phonemes of the message. I hand selected the icons in this app from The Noun Project, trying to cover most common short words and word parts.


Rebus Chat was made for the course Interactive Art, and you can read more about the design process and rebus-ification algorithm in this blog post.

An example of a rebus-ified message: "I am typing something for this demo" became "Eye M tie pin sum thin four th+S day mow."
An example of rebus-ified messages: the first person said "On top of the world" and the other replied "but underneath the ocean." Since the app ignores breaks between words, "but undern..." because "button + d + urn..." in the final puzzle. The algorithm also allows for imperfect matches, as demonstrated by "world" turning into "well + d."
