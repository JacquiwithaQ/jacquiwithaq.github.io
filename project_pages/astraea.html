A Daydream VR app for drawing new constellations with real stars.
Astraea is a virtual reality stargazing app made for Google Daydream. It places the user in an open field beneath a starry sky, using a green laser pointer to interact with the stars above them. When they hover over a star, it swells and displays its name. When they click on a series of stars, a line is drawn connecting them. In this way, the user can draw anything they want in the sky, creating original constellations for their personal mythology.


Astraea was made for the course Interactive Art, taught by professor Golan Levin. The stars' positions and magnitudes are populated from The HYG Database. You can read more about Astraea in this blog post.
