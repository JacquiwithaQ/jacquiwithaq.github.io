A phonetic writing system with moving parts.
Timescript is a decorative writing system where time (ie. movement) is a critical feature. It encodes the sounds used to say a phrase, rather than the letters used to spell it, in an infinitely looping animation that cannot be read when paused. Contiguous moving parts represent syllables, ordered by the period of their animation (fastest to slowest). Each symbol on a moving part represents a consonant sound, while their collective movement indicates the vowel sound. The "calligrapher" can arrange specific pieces as they see fit.
