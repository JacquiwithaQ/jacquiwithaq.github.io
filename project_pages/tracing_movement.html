Tracing Movement
2016 · Processing, Axidraw Robot, Watercolor
Watercolor paintings that trace the paths of a person's limbs through different physical activities.
Tracing Movement is a computational art project that turns motion capture data into watercolor paintings. First a person performs a short action while the locations of their body parts are recorded (as BVH data). Then a robot uses a paintbrush to retrace the positions of their wrists and ankles, with a different color for each limb. The result is an abstract painting that shows the general flow of the recorded motion without actually depicitng a human at all. I created a short series of paintings this way, representing movements from martial arts, gymnastics, and ballet dance.


This project was made for the course Interactivity and Computation, taught by Professor Golan Levin. It uses motion capture performances from Katia Villevald, Max Mirho, Sheela Hanagal, Wynne Yao, and Aria Paradkar. The mocap data was collected using a Microsoft Kinect and Brekel, and it was painted by an AxiDraw. This work was inspired by Lésia Trubat's Traces. You can read more about my process in this blog post about the project.
