A messaging app that translates all text into rebuses (picture puns/puzzles).
Rebus Chat is an Android messaging app that automatically translates every message into a rebus: a puzzle in which words are conveyed by combinations of pictures and letters. For example, the word "here" in a rebus might be represented by the letter H, a plus sign, and a picture of an ear. As the user types into Rebus Chat, their message is broken down into its phonemes (pronunciation sounds) and a dynamic programming algorithmm searches for the rebus that matches it best. This updates in real time as the user makes changes, until they are satisfied with their rebus message and send it to be deciphered by the recipient.


Rebus Chat was made for the course Interactive Art, taught by Professor Golan Levin. Its word pronunciations are from the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary and most icons are from The Noun Project. You can read more about the design process and rebus-ification algorithm in this blog post.

An example of a rebus-ified message: "I am typing something for this demo" became "Eye M tie pin sum thin four th+S day mow."
