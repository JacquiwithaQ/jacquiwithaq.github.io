A series of proofs generated by an LSTM trained on CS course content.
Infinite Descent was inspired by my experience as a teaching assistant for the course 15-251, Great Ideas in Theoretical Computer Science. I trained an LSTM network on all of the 15-251 course content I could find, including course notes, homework assignments, and practice problems. Then I had it generate a large amount of text. Half of this I transcribed by hand to resemble the proofs that students submit for homework and TAs must grade. The other half I formatted to resemble the course notes that students study to learn the material. In both situations, the reader is highly motivated to understand what is written. I invite viewers of the piece to step into one of these roles, and experience the frustration of not being able to fully comprehend the text in front of them. Infinite Descent was made for the course Art and Machine Learning, taught by Professors Eunsu Kang and Barnabas Poczos.
